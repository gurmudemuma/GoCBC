# Ethiopian Coffee Export Workflow - Proper Sequence Enforcement

## Overview
This document defines the **EXACT sequence** of steps in the coffee export workflow, including when documents must be submitted, when actions can occur, and what prerequisites must be met at each stage.

---

## 🎯 MASTER WORKFLOW SEQUENCE

```
STEP 1: Exporter Registration (Public → ECTA)
   ↓
STEP 2: Contract Registration (Exporter → NBE)
   ↓
STEP 3: Letter of Credit Issuance (Banks)
   ↓
STEP 4: Forex Allocation (NBE)
   ↓
STEP 5: Shipment Creation (Exporter)
   ↓
STEP 6: Quality Inspection (ECTA)
   ↓
STEP 7: Export Permit Issuance (ECTA)
   ↓
STEP 8: Customs Clearance (Customs)
   ↓
STEP 9: Shipping & Logistics (Shipping Company)
   ↓
STEP 10: Payment Document Submission (Exporter → Bank) ⚠️ CRITICAL TIMING
   ↓
STEP 11: Document Verification (Bank)
   ↓
STEP 12: SWIFT Payment (Foreign Bank → Ethiopian Bank)
   ↓
STEP 13: Payment Settlement (Ethiopian Bank)
```

---

## 📋 STEP-BY-STEP DETAILED SEQUENCE

### **STEP 1: EXPORTER REGISTRATION**

**Who:** Prospective Exporter  
**Portal:** Public Registration Form (`/register-exporter`)  
**Prerequisites:** NONE (Entry point)

**Documents Required at This Stage:**
1. ✅ **Business License** (Upload PDF)
2. ✅ **TIN Certificate** (Tax Identification Number)
3. ✅ **Professional Taster Certificate** (Certificate showing qualified Q-grader)
4. ✅ **Laboratory Facility Documents** (If owned) OR **Lab Service Contract** (If contracted)
5. ✅ **Capital Evidence** (Bank statement showing minimum 50,000,000 ETB)
6. ✅ **Company Registration Certificate**

**Actions:**
- Fill application form
- Upload required documents
- Submit for ECTA review

**Status:** `PENDING`

**Next Step Trigger:** ECTA approves application

---

### **STEP 2: ECTA APPROVAL & BANK ASSIGNMENT**

**Who:** ECTA Official  
**Portal:** ECTA Portal → Pending Applications  
**Prerequisites:** Exporter has submitted complete application

**Documents Reviewed:**
- All documents from Step 1
- Capital requirement verification
- Professional qualifications check

**Actions:**
1. Review application documents
2. Verify capital requirement (min 50M ETB)
3. Verify professional taster certification
4. **SELECT BANK & BRANCH** for LC processing
   - Bank: e.g., "Commercial Bank of Ethiopia"
   - Branch: e.g., "Bole Branch (CBE-002)"
5. Auto-generate:
   - Exporter ID (e.g., EXP7419517)
   - ECTA License Number (e.g., ECTA-LIC-2026-170)
   - License Expiry Date (1 year from approval)
6. Approve or Reject

**Status After Approval:** `APPROVED`, User account `ACTIVE`

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **ECTA Export License** (Generated by system)
- ✅ **Login Credentials** (Sent via email)

**Next Step Trigger:** Exporter receives credentials and can login

---

### **STEP 3: CONTRACT REGISTRATION**

**Who:** Exporter  
**Portal:** Exporter Portal → My Contracts → Create Contract  
**Prerequisites:** 
- Exporter account ACTIVE
- ECTA license VALID (not expired, not suspended)

**Documents Required at This Stage:**
1. ✅ **Sales Contract with Buyer** (Signed agreement)
2. ✅ **Buyer's Bank Details** (Name, BIC/SWIFT code, address)
3. ✅ **Pro Forma Invoice** (Preliminary invoice)

**Actions:**
1. Enter buyer information
2. Enter coffee details (type, quantity, price)
3. Specify delivery terms (FOB/CIF)
4. Specify ports (loading/discharge)
5. **Declare EUDR Compliance** (if EU destination)
6. Upload sales contract document
7. Submit for NBE approval

**System Auto-Fills:**
- Exporter's Bank (from profile)
- Exporter's Bank Branch (from profile)

**Status:** `REGISTERED`

**Next Step Trigger:** NBE approves contract

---

### **STEP 4: NBE CONTRACT APPROVAL**

**Who:** NBE Official  
**Portal:** NBE Portal → Contract Approvals  
**Prerequisites:** Contract registered by exporter

**Documents Reviewed:**
- Sales contract
- Pro forma invoice
- Buyer information

**Actions:**
1. Review contract details
2. Verify coffee price (minimum price compliance)
3. Verify quantity limits
4. Check buyer's country (sanctions compliance)
5. Verify forex requirements
6. Approve or Reject

**Status After Approval:** `NBE_APPROVED` or `APPROVED`

**Next Step Trigger:** Contract approved → Bank can issue LC

---

### **STEP 5: LETTER OF CREDIT (LC) REQUEST & ISSUANCE**

**Who:** Exporter (requests) → Bank (issues)  
**Portal:** Banks Portal → LC Management  
**Prerequisites:** 
- Contract status: `NBE_APPROVED`
- Exporter has valid license

**Documents Required at This Stage:**
1. ✅ **NBE-Approved Sales Contract** (from Step 4)
2. ✅ **Pro Forma Invoice**
3. ✅ **LC Application Form** (Bank's form)
4. ✅ **Exporter's Bank Account Details**

**Actions (by Bank):**
1. Exporter contacts assigned bank branch
2. Branch reviews NBE-approved contract
3. Bank creates LC request in system
4. Branch manager approves LC
5. Bank issues LC to buyer's bank (SWIFT MT700)

**Status:** `REQUESTED` → `APPROVED` → `ISSUED`

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **Letter of Credit** (SWIFT MT700 message)

**Next Step Trigger:** LC issued → NBE can allocate forex

---

### **STEP 6: FOREX ALLOCATION**

**Who:** NBE Official  
**Portal:** NBE Portal → Forex Management  
**Prerequisites:** LC status: `ISSUED`

**Documents Required:** NONE (Auto-triggered by LC issuance)

**Actions:**
1. View issued LCs
2. Allocate forex to exporter
3. Set exchange rate
4. Set retention rate (typically 40% USD, 60% ETB)
5. Link allocation to LC

**Status:** `ALLOCATED`

**Next Step Trigger:** Forex allocated → Exporter can create shipment

---

### **STEP 7: SHIPMENT CREATION**

**Who:** Exporter  
**Portal:** Exporter Portal → Shipments → Create Shipment  
**Prerequisites:** 
- Contract approved
- LC issued
- Coffee available for export

**Documents Required at This Stage:**
1. ✅ **ECX Purchase Receipt** (if buying from ECX)
2. ✅ **Warehouse Receipt** (coffee storage proof)
3. ✅ **ICO Certificate of Origin** (ICO number)

**Actions:**
1. Select approved contract
2. Enter shipment details:
   - Coffee quantity (kg)
   - Coffee type and grade
   - Origin (region/farm)
   - ICO number
   - ECX lot number (if applicable)
   - Export channel (Direct/ECX/Union)
3. Declare EUDR compliance coordinates
4. Submit for quality inspection

**Status:** `CREATED`

**Next Step Trigger:** Shipment created → ECTA can inspect

---

### **STEP 8: QUALITY INSPECTION**

**Who:** ECTA Quality Control Officer  
**Portal:** ECTA Portal → Quality Control → Inspection Workflow  
**Prerequisites:** Shipment status: `CREATED`

**Documents Required:** NONE (Physical coffee inspection)

**Actions:**
1. **Physical Inspection:**
   - Defect count (primary/secondary)
   - Moisture content
   - Screen size analysis
   - Bean appearance

2. **Cupping/Sensory Evaluation:**
   - Fragrance/Aroma (10 points)
   - Flavor (10 points)
   - Acidity (10 points)
   - Body (10 points)
   - Aftertaste (10 points)
   - Balance, Uniformity, Clean Cup, Sweetness
   - Calculate final cupping score

3. **Grade Determination:**
   - System auto-calculates grade based on score
   - Q-Grader reviews and confirms

4. **Approval:**
   - Manager reviews inspection
   - Approve or Reject

**Status:** `CREATED` → `QUALITY_APPROVED`

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **Quality Inspection Report** (with cupping scores)
- ✅ **Quality Certificate** (Grade certificate)

**Next Step Trigger:** Quality approved → ECTA can issue export permit

---

### **STEP 9: EXPORT PERMIT ISSUANCE**

**Who:** ECTA Permit Officer  
**Portal:** ECTA Portal → Quality Control → Issue Permit  
**Prerequisites:** Shipment status: `QUALITY_APPROVED`

**Documents Required:** NONE (Based on inspection results)

**Actions:**
1. Review quality inspection results
2. Generate export permit number
3. Issue export permit
4. System records permit details

**Status:** `QUALITY_APPROVED` → `PERMIT_ISSUED`

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **ECTA Export Permit** (Official export authorization)

**Next Step Trigger:** Permit issued → Customs can clear

---

### **STEP 10: CUSTOMS CLEARANCE**

**Who:** Customs Officer  
**Portal:** Customs Portal → Export Declarations  
**Prerequisites:** Shipment status: `PERMIT_ISSUED`

**Documents Required at This Stage:**
1. ✅ **ECTA Export Permit** (from Step 9)
2. ✅ **Quality Certificate** (from Step 8)
3. ✅ **Sales Contract** (from Step 3)
4. ✅ **Commercial Invoice** (Exporter provides)
5. ✅ **Packing List** (Exporter provides)
6. ✅ **Certificate of Origin** (ICO certificate)
7. ✅ **Phytosanitary Certificate** (Plant health clearance)
8. ✅ **EUDR Due Diligence Statement** (if EU destination)

**Actions:**
1. Create customs declaration
2. Physical inspection of coffee
3. Documentary review:
   - Verify export permit
   - Verify quality certificate
   - Check invoice against contract
   - Verify weight and packaging
4. EUDR compliance check (if applicable)
5. Calculate duties (if any)
6. Issue customs clearance

**Status:** `PERMIT_ISSUED` → `CUSTOMS_CLEARED`

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **Customs Clearance Certificate**
- ✅ **Customs Declaration Form**

**Next Step Trigger:** Customs cleared → Shipping company can load

---

### **STEP 11: SHIPPING & LOGISTICS**

**Who:** Shipping Company  
**Portal:** Shipping Portal → Container Management  
**Prerequisites:** Shipment status: `CUSTOMS_CLEARED`

**Documents Required at This Stage:**
1. ✅ **Customs Clearance Certificate** (from Step 10)
2. ✅ **Export Permit** (from Step 9)
3. ✅ **Commercial Invoice**
4. ✅ **Packing List**
5. ✅ **Booking Confirmation** (Vessel/container booking)

**Actions:**
1. Book container (dry or reefer)
2. Assign vessel
3. Load coffee at port
4. Generate Bill of Lading (B/L)
5. Attach IoT sensors (GPS, temperature, humidity)
6. Depart from port
7. Track in transit
8. Arrive at destination

**Status:** `CUSTOMS_CLEARED` → `BOOKED` → `LOADED` → `DEPARTED` → `IN_TRANSIT` → `ARRIVED` → `DELIVERED`

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **Bill of Lading (B/L)** ⚠️ **CRITICAL FOR PAYMENT**
- ✅ **Container Loading Report**
- ✅ **IoT Tracking Data**

**Next Step Trigger:** Goods loaded & departed → Exporter can submit payment documents

---

### **⚠️ STEP 12: PAYMENT DOCUMENT SUBMISSION** (CRITICAL TIMING)

**Who:** Exporter  
**Portal:** Exporter Portal → Payments → Submit Documents  
**Prerequisites:** 
- Shipment status: `DEPARTED` or `IN_TRANSIT`
- Bill of Lading issued
- All export documents obtained

**⏰ TIMING REQUIREMENT:**
- **MUST submit within LC validity period** (typically 21 days after shipment date)
- **MUST submit before LC expiry date**
- Earlier submission = Faster payment

**Documents Required at This Stage (COMPLETE SET):**

1. ✅ **Bill of Lading (B/L)** ⚠️ **MOST CRITICAL**
   - Original, signed by carrier
   - Dated (must be within LC validity)
   - Clean B/L (no damage clauses)
   - Shows correct quantity
   - Shows correct destination

2. ✅ **Commercial Invoice**
   - Issued by exporter
   - Shows correct amount (matching LC)
   - Shows coffee description
   - Buyer and seller details

3. ✅ **Certificate of Origin (ICO)**
   - Ethiopian origin certification
   - ICO stamp
   - Shows exporter details

4. ✅ **Quality Certificates**
   - ECTA quality inspection report
   - Cupping scores
   - Grade certificate

5. ✅ **Phytosanitary Certificate**
   - Plant health clearance
   - Ministry of Agriculture stamp
   - Valid for destination country

6. ✅ **Packing List**
   - Detailed list of bags/containers
   - Weight per bag
   - Total net and gross weight
   - Marks and numbers

7. ✅ **Insurance Certificate** (if required by LC)
   - Cargo insurance proof
   - Coverage amount
   - Valid for shipment

8. ✅ **ECTA Export Permit**
   - Official export authorization
   - Permit number

9. ✅ **Customs Declaration**
   - Customs clearance proof
   - Declaration number

10. ✅ **EUDR Due Diligence Statement** (if EU destination)
    - Geo-coordinates of origin
    - Deforestation risk assessment
    - Compliance declaration

**Actions:**
1. Gather all required documents
2. Scan/digitize documents
3. Upload to system
4. Submit to assigned bank branch
5. Await bank verification

**Status:** `DEPARTED` → Payment status: `DOCUMENTS_SUBMITTED`

**⚠️ CRITICAL RULES:**
- **All documents MUST be presented together** (UCP 600 rule)
- **Documents MUST be consistent** (same quantities, dates, descriptions)
- **B/L date MUST be before LC expiry**
- **No discrepancies allowed** (strict compliance)

**Next Step Trigger:** Documents submitted → Bank reviews

---

### **STEP 13: DOCUMENT VERIFICATION BY BANK**

**Who:** Bank Officer (at assigned branch)  
**Portal:** Banks Portal → Payment Processing → Verify Documents  
**Prerequisites:** Payment status: `DOCUMENTS_SUBMITTED`

**Documents Reviewed:** All documents from Step 12

**⏰ TIMING:** Bank has **5-7 banking days** to review (UCP 600 standard)

**Actions (UCP 600 Compliance Check):**

1. **Completeness Check:**
   - All required documents present?
   - Correct number of originals?
   - All documents signed?

2. **Consistency Check:**
   - Quantities match across all documents?
   - Descriptions consistent?
   - Dates logical and sequential?
   - Beneficiary name matches?

3. **LC Terms Compliance:**
   - Amount matches LC amount?
   - Shipment date within LC validity?
   - Documents presented within LC validity?
   - Origin matches LC requirement?
   - Quality grade meets LC requirement?

4. **Document Authenticity:**
   - Signatures valid?
   - Stamps genuine?
   - No alterations or corrections?

5. **B/L Specific Checks:**
   - Clean B/L (no clauses)?
   - Dated before LC expiry?
   - Shows correct port of loading?
   - Shows correct port of discharge?
   - Freight paid or collect as per LC?

**Decision:**
- ✅ **APPROVE:** All documents comply → Process payment
- ❌ **REJECT:** Discrepancies found → Notify exporter

**Status:** `DOCUMENTS_SUBMITTED` → `VERIFIED` (if approved)

**If Discrepancies Found:**
- Bank notifies exporter of issues
- Exporter must correct and resubmit
- **Time is critical** - LC may expire!

**Next Step Trigger:** Documents verified → Bank initiates SWIFT payment

---

### **STEP 14: SWIFT PAYMENT INITIATION**

**Who:** Buyer's Bank (Foreign Bank)  
**Portal:** Banks Portal → SWIFT Management  
**Prerequisites:** Documents verified by advising bank (exporter's bank)

**Documents Sent to Buyer's Bank:** All verified documents from Step 12

**Actions (by Buyer's Bank):**
1. Receive document notification from advising bank
2. Review documents
3. Debit buyer's account (or utilize LC)
4. Initiate SWIFT payment
5. Send SWIFT MT103 message

**SWIFT Message Details:**
```
Message Type: MT103 (Customer Credit Transfer)
Sender: Buyer's Bank (e.g., SCBLGB2L)
Receiver: Exporter's Bank (e.g., CBETETAA)
Receiver Branch: Specific branch (e.g., Bole Branch, CBE-002)
Amount: USD 500,000.00 (example)
Currency: USD
Value Date: 2026-06-28 (example)
Beneficiary: Exporter Name
Beneficiary Account: Account number at branch
Reference: LC number, Contract number
```

**Status:** Payment: `SWIFT_INITIATED`

**⏰ TIMING:** Typically **2-5 business days** for international transfer

**Next Step Trigger:** SWIFT message sent → Ethiopian bank receives

---

### **STEP 15: SWIFT RECEIPT CONFIRMATION**

**Who:** Ethiopian Bank (Assigned Branch)  
**Portal:** Banks Portal → SWIFT Tracking  
**Prerequisites:** SWIFT message sent by foreign bank

**Actions:**
1. Receive SWIFT MT103 message
2. Verify SWIFT reference
3. Verify amount matches LC
4. Verify beneficiary account
5. Confirm receipt in system
6. Notify exporter

**Status:** `SWIFT_INITIATED` → `SWIFT_RECEIVED`

**Next Step Trigger:** SWIFT received → Bank processes settlement

---

### **STEP 16: PAYMENT SETTLEMENT** (FINAL STEP)

**Who:** Ethiopian Bank (Assigned Branch)  
**Portal:** Banks Portal → Settlement  
**Prerequisites:** Payment status: `SWIFT_RECEIVED`

**Actions:**

1. **Apply NBE Retention Policy:**
   ```
   Total Amount: USD 500,000.00 (example)
   
   Retention Rate: 40% (NBE policy)
   Retained Amount: USD 200,000.00 (kept in forex account)
   
   Conversion Rate: 60% (NBE policy)
   Converted Amount: USD 300,000.00 (to be converted)
   
   Exchange Rate: 120.50 ETB/USD (example)
   Amount in Birr: ETB 36,150,000.00
   ```

2. **Credit Exporter Accounts:**
   - **Forex Account:** + USD 200,000.00
   - **Birr Account:** + ETB 36,150,000.00

3. **Record Settlement:**
   - Settlement date
   - SWIFT reference
   - NBE approval reference
   - Exchange rate used
   - Amounts credited

4. **Generate Reports:**
   - Settlement advice to exporter
   - NBE forex report
   - Internal bank records

**Status:** `SWIFT_RECEIVED` → `SETTLED` ✅

**Documents Generated:**
- ✅ **Settlement Advice** (Bank statement showing credit)
- ✅ **Forex Retention Report** (For NBE)
- ✅ **Payment Receipt** (Official receipt)

**🎉 WORKFLOW COMPLETE!**

## 🚫 WORKFLOW ENFORCEMENT RULES

### **Cannot Proceed If:**

1. **Cannot Register Contract if:**
   - Exporter license expired
   - Exporter license suspended
   - Exporter account inactive

2. **Cannot Request LC if:**
   - Contract not NBE-approved
   - Exporter has outstanding violations
   - Contract amount exceeds exporter's capital capacity

3. **Cannot Create Shipment if:**
   - No approved contract
   - No issued LC
   - Exporter license issues

4. **Cannot Inspect if:**
   - Shipment not created
   - Coffee not physically available

5. **Cannot Issue Permit if:**
   - Quality not approved
   - Inspection incomplete

6. **Cannot Clear Customs if:**
   - No export permit
   - Missing required documents
   - EUDR non-compliance (for EU destinations)

7. **Cannot Load Ship if:**
   - Not customs cleared
   - Missing shipping documents

8. **Cannot Submit Payment Documents if:**
   - Shipment not departed
   - B/L not issued
   - Documents incomplete

9. **Cannot Verify Documents if:**
   - Documents not submitted
   - LC expired
   - Presentation period passed

10. **Cannot Initiate SWIFT if:**
    - Documents not verified
    - Discrepancies found
    - Buyer hasn't authorized payment

11. **Cannot Settle Payment if:**
    - SWIFT not received
    - NBE forex allocation not available
    - Account discrepancies

## ⏰ CRITICAL TIMING POINTS

### **Time-Sensitive Actions:**

1. **LC Validity Period:**
   - Typically **90-180 days** from issuance
   - Shipment MUST occur before LC expiry
   - Documents MUST be presented before LC expiry

2. **Document Presentation Period:**
   - Typically **21 days after shipment date**
   - Some LCs allow **shorter period (10-15 days)**
   - **MUST present within period or lose payment!**

3. **Bank Review Period:**
   - Banks have **5-7 banking days** to review (UCP 600)
   - Can be shorter if discrepancies found

4. **SWIFT Transfer Time:**
   - International transfer: **2-5 business days**
   - Can be faster with correspondent banking

5. **Settlement Time:**
   - **Same day or next business day** after SWIFT receipt
   - NBE retention applied immediately

### **Exporter's Critical Deadline:**

```
Shipment Date: June 20, 2026
         ↓
Documents MUST be submitted by: July 11, 2026 (21 days)
         ↓
LC Expiry Date: August 15, 2026
         ↓
If documents NOT submitted by July 11:
   → LATE PRESENTATION
   → Bank may reject
   → Need buyer's authorization to accept late documents
   → RISK: May not get paid!
```

## ✅ SYSTEM ENFORCEMENT

### **Status-Based Enforcement:**

The system enforces proper workflow sequence by checking status prerequisites:

```typescript
// Example: Cannot submit payment documents if shipment not departed
if (shipment.status !== 'DEPARTED' && shipment.status !== 'IN_TRANSIT') {
  throw new Error('Cannot submit payment documents. Shipment must depart first.');
}

// Example: Cannot verify documents if LC expired
if (new Date() > new Date(lc.expiryDate)) {
  throw new Error('Cannot verify documents. LC has expired.');
}

// Example: Cannot settle payment if documents not verified
if (payment.status !== 'SWIFT_RECEIVED') {
  throw new Error('Cannot settle payment. SWIFT message not received.');
}
```

### **UI Enforcement:**

Buttons and actions disabled until prerequisites met:

```typescript
// Submit Documents button
<Button
  disabled={shipment.status !== 'DEPARTED' && shipment.status !== 'IN_TRANSIT'}
  onClick={handleSubmitDocuments}
>
  Submit Payment Documents
</Button>

// Only show when appropriate status
{shipment.status === 'DEPARTED' && (
  <PaymentDocuments shipmentId={shipment.id} />
)}
```
