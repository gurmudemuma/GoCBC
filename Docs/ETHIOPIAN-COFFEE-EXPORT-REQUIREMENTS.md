# Ethiopian Coffee Export Requirements - Verified Information

**Based on:** Official sources, ECTA regulations, NBE directives  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Purpose:** Document actual Ethiopian coffee export requirements

## 🏛️ **Regulatory Framework**

### **Key Authorities:**

1. **ECTA** - Ethiopian Coffee & Tea Authority (est. 2016)
   - Coffee export licensing
   - Quality control and grading
   - Laboratory certification
   - Professional taster certification

2. **NBE** - National Bank of Ethiopia
   - Sales contract registration
   - Forex allocation
   - Foreign currency retention (40%)
   - Minimum price enforcement

3. **ESWS** - Ethiopian Single Window Service (esw.et)
   - Trade license management
   - Import/export permits
   - Customs declarations
   - Inter-agency coordination

4. **Ethiopian Customs Commission**
   - Export clearance
   - EUDR compliance verification
   - Physical inspection

## 📋 **Known Export Requirements**

### **1. ECTA Coffee Export License**

**Capital Requirements:**
- **Exporters:** Minimum capital requirement (varies by exporter type)
- **Recent Changes:** Ethiopia has "drastically raised capital requirements" (2025)
- **Farmer Exporters:** Different/lower requirements

**Quality Requirements:**
- **Certified Laboratory:** ECTA-certified coffee laboratory required for basic quality testing
  - Exemption: Farmer exporters
- **Professional Taster:** Required qualifications:
  - Minimum: Diploma-level qualification
  - Must have: Renewed proficiency certificate
  - Restriction: Can serve only ONE coffee dispatcher
  - Employment: Full-time appointment required

**License Details:**
- Issued by ECTA
- Annual renewal required
- Subject to suspension for violations
- Required for export permit issuance

### **2. NBE Sales Contract Registration**

**Minimum Price Requirement:**
- Exporters must sell at or above NBE-set minimum price
- If contract doesn't meet minimum: NBE will NOT register it
- Without NBE registration: Cannot obtain export permits
- Result: Impossible to ship coffee

**Contract Registration Process:**
- Submit sales contract to NBE
- NBE reviews pricing against minimums
- NBE registers contract (issues reference number)
- Contract registration required for:
  - Forex allocation
  - Export permit
  - Customs clearance

### **3. Foreign Exchange Regulations (NBE Directive FXD/01/2024)**

**Forex Retention Policy:**
- **Goods Exporters:** Can retain 40% of export earnings in foreign currency
- **Service Exporters:** Can retain 100% of export earnings
- **Mandatory Conversion:** 60% must be converted to Ethiopian Birr (ETB) at official rate
- **Retention Account:** Banks provide foreign currency retention accounts
- **Recent History:**
  - Previously: 20% retention
  - August 2023: Increased to 40%
  - Current: 40% for goods exporters

**Purpose of 40% Retention:**
- Allows importers to access forex for business operations
- Reduces reliance on banking system for forex allocation
- Incentivizes export performance

### **4. Trade License & TIN**

**Required from Ministry of Trade:**
- Business trade license
- TIN (Tax Identification Number) certificate
- Business registration certificate
- VAT registration (if applicable)

**ESWS Registration:**
- Mandatory registration on esw.et platform
- Submit all trade-related applications electronically
- Receive ESWS user ID

### **5. Quality Control (ECTA)**

**Government QC Checkpoint:**
- All coffee passes through ECTA quality control before export approval
- Purpose: Protects "Ethiopia" brand reputation
- Process: 
  - Lots graded
  - Those not meeting grade requirements: rejected or downgraded
  - Adds time to export process

**Required Documents:**
- Quality certificate from ECTA
- Laboratory test results
- Professional taster certification

### **6. Customs & Logistics**

**Export Documentation (via ESWS):**
- Customs declaration
- Export permit
- ECTA quality certificate
- NBE contract registration
- Bill of lading
- Certificate of origin
- Commercial invoice
- Packing list
- Insurance certificate

**Physical Process:**
- Coffee transported to Addis Ababa
- ECTA quality inspection
- Customs clearance
- Transport to Djibouti port (landlocked country)
- Container shipping
