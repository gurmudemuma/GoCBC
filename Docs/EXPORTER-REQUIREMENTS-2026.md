# Ethiopian Coffee Exporter Registration Requirements - 2026

## Overview
This document outlines the official requirements for registering as a coffee exporter in Ethiopia as of 2026, based on **ECTA Directive 1106/2025**.

**Source:** [Capital Ethiopia - Ethiopia drastically raises capital requirements for coffee exporters](https://capitalethiopia.com/2025/09/14/ethiopia-drastically-raises-capital-requirements-for-coffee-exporters/)

---

## 1. Capital Requirements (Drastically Increased)

### Private Exporters
- **Minimum Capital:** 15,000,000 ETB (Ethiopian Birr)
- **Previous Requirement:** 1,000,000 ETB
- **Increase:** 1,400% increase (15x higher)

### Trade Associations & Companies
(Joint Stock Companies, Limited Liability Companies)
- **Minimum Capital:** 20,000,000 ETB
- **Previous Requirement:** 1,500,000 ETB
- **Increase:** 1,233% increase (13.3x higher)

### Individual Exporters (with Competency Certification)
- **Minimum Capital:** 10,000,000 ETB
- **Previous Requirement:** 1,000,000 ETB
- **Increase:** 900% increase (10x higher)

### Foreign Investors (Raw Coffee Exports)
**Source:** [U.S. Trade.gov - Ethiopia Allows Foreign Investors in Trade](https://www.trade.gov/market-intelligence/ethiopia-allows-foreign-investors-trade)
- **Previous Procurement:** Must have procured at least $10 million USD annually from Ethiopia for the past 3 years
- **Annual Commitment:** Must agree to export at least $10 million USD within the permit year
- **Capital:** USD 2.5 million paid-up capital minimum

## 2. Professional Coffee Taster (Mandatory)

### Requirements:
- ✅ **Education:** Minimum diploma qualification
- ✅ **Certificate:** Must possess a renewed/valid proficiency certificate
- ✅ **Exclusivity:** One taster can serve **only one coffee dispatcher**
- ✅ **Renewal:** Certificate must be current and renewed regularly

### Purpose:
Professional tasters ensure quality control and maintain Ethiopia's coffee reputation in international markets.

---

## 3. Coffee Laboratory Facility (Mandatory)

### Requirements:
- ✅ **ECTA Certification:** Laboratory must be certified by ECTA
- ✅ **Testing Capability:** Must perform basic quality testing
- ✅ **Standards:** Must meet ECTA standards for coffee grading and analysis

### Exemptions:
- ❌ **Farmer Exporters:** Exempt from laboratory requirement
- ✅ All other exporter types: **Mandatory**

### Options:
1. **Own Laboratory:** Company owns and operates ECTA-certified lab
2. **Contracted Laboratory:** Agreement with ECTA-certified third-party lab

### Testing Requirements:
- Physical analysis (bean size, moisture content, defects)
- Sensory evaluation (cupping tests)
- Basic chemical analysis
- Grade determination according to Ethiopian standards

---

## 4. Business Registration Requirements

### Basic Documents:
1. **TIN (Tax Identification Number)** - Issued by Ethiopian Revenue and Customs Authority (ERCA)
2. **Business License** - From Ministry of Trade and Regional Integration
3. **Company Registration Certificate** - Commercial Registration and Business Licensing Agency
4. **Bank Account** - Verified Ethiopian bank account

### Additional Documents:
- Proof of capital (bank statements, financial audit)
- Company articles of association (for companies)
- Shareholder agreements
- Office/facility lease or ownership documents

---

## 5. Registration Process

### Step 1: Document Preparation
- Gather all required legal documents
- Secure proof of capital requirement
- Obtain/verify professional taster certificate
- Secure laboratory certification or contract

### Step 2: Online Application
- Submit application through CECBS portal: http://localhost:3000/register-exporter
- Provide all company information
- Upload supporting documents
- Pay application fees

### Step 3: ECTA Review
- **Document Verification:** 2-3 business days
- **Facility Inspection:** ECTA may conduct site visit
- **Compliance Check:** Verify all requirements are met

### Step 4: License Issuance
- **Approval:** ECTA issues export license
- **License Number:** Format ECTA-LIC-YYYY-XXX
- **Validity:** Typically 1 year, renewable
- **Blockchain Registration:** Exporter registered on CECBS blockchain

### Step 5: Portal Access
- Receive login credentials
- Access Exporter Portal
- Begin export operations

## 6. Rationale for Increased Requirements

According to industry sources, ECTA raised these requirements to:

1. **Quality Control:** Ensure only serious, well-capitalized exporters operate
2. **Market Reputation:** Protect Ethiopia's coffee brand internationally
3. **Compliance:** Meet international quality and traceability standards
4. **EUDR Readiness:** Prepare for EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) requirements
5. **Professionalization:** Elevate the industry to international standards
6. **Financial Stability:** Ensure exporters can fulfill contracts and obligations

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can existing exporters continue operating?
**A:** Yes, but they must upgrade their capital and obtain required certifications within the transition period.

### Q: What if I can't afford the new capital requirements?
**A:** Consider forming a cooperative, seeking investors, or partnering with other exporters to meet requirements.

### Q: Is the laboratory requirement negotiable?
**A:** No, except for farmer exporters. All commercial exporters must have access to an ECTA-certified laboratory.

### Q: Can one company employ multiple professional tasters?
**A:** Yes, but each taster can only serve one coffee dispatcher/exporter at a time.

### Q: How long does the application process take?
**A:** Typically 2-4 weeks, depending on document completeness and site inspection scheduling.

### Q: Can foreign investors become exporters?
**A:** Yes, with minimum $10M annual procurement history and $2.5M capital investment.

   - Business license
   - Bank account details
   - Contact information
